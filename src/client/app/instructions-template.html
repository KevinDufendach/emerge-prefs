You have been invited to participate in a research study that gives you the option to learn your genetic results for some health conditions. Some of the conditions are rare, meaning less than 200,000 people in the United States are living with the condition. Some of the rare conditions, like cystic fibrosis, are caused by gene variations inherited from each parent. Other conditions, such as cancer and heart disease are more common and can be caused by environmental, lifestyle and/or genetic factors. For example, most risk factors for heart disease are not genetic. In this study, it is possible to learn some gene results that are known to increase the risk for heart disease. However, if gene variations are not found that increase the risk for heart disease, you may still be at risk for the condition because of all the other factors that can cause heart disease. Any gene variation found in you may or may not be present in other family members. In addition, there may be gene variations in you or your family members that are not part of the genetic test being used in this study.

The following questions will help you decide what types of genetic research results you would like to receive. For this study, all the genetic results that you can learn about are for conditions that you or other family members could develop. Some conditions may or may not be preventable and some conditions may or may not be treatable.

High cholesterol is an example of a health condition that can be inherited and can begin in childhood or adulthood. Inherited high cholesterol can lead to heart attacks before the age of 40. Heart attacks from high cholesterol are preventable if diet and exercise can keep cholesterol at normal levels. High cholesterol can also be treated with medications. Therefore, inherited high cholesterol is an example of a preventable and treatable condition. You will receive a list of the conditions that match your choices for this study after you answer the questions below.
